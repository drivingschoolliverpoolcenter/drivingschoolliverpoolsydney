About Ashraf

Safe habits first, licence results second.

Ashraf teaches from Liverpool with a clear focus on long-term skill building. The aim is not only to help a student pass a test, but to help them make better decisions at roundabouts, school zones, busy intersections, motorway entries and local suburban streets for years to come.

That is why each lesson is structured, local and practical. Instead of filling time, lessons focus on the habits that matter most: observation, speed control, spacing, parking judgement, calm decision-making and repeatable routines that hold up under pressure.

In South West Sydney, learners come from different backgrounds, speak different first languages and learn at different speeds. Ashraf meets each student where they are, whether they are a complete beginner in Liverpool, a nervous learner near Casula, a parent-supported teen in Prestons or a returning adult driver working around Chipping Norton and Moorebank.

  • Lessons built around real Liverpool roads, not generic routes.
  • Safe habits that stay useful well beyond the driving test.
  • Warm, direct coaching that works for nervous learners and confident learners alike.

Driving approach

Cleaner habits, calmer lessons and less wasted driving time.

Good driving instruction should also teach better road behaviour. That includes smoother control, fewer rushed decisions and more purposeful lesson planning.

Purposeful local routes

Lessons are planned around the local roads and manoeuvres the learner actually needs, which helps avoid pointless driving and keeps lesson time useful.

Smoother vehicle control

Steady braking, measured acceleration and better observation do not just improve safety, they also reduce the stop-start stress that makes new drivers more anxious.

Lower-idle lesson habits

Clear explanations before the manoeuvre and focused repetition after it help keep lessons moving without long stretches of unnecessary idling.

Vehicle care and road respect

Learners are taught to treat the car, the road space and other drivers responsibly, which is part of becoming a safer long-term driver.
