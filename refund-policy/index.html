Policy overview

Not every refund request is treated the same, and that needs to be clear up front.

Most lesson bookings go ahead without any issue, but refund requests can still arise. This page exists so learners and parents understand how those requests are approached before they pay for lessons or packages.

The structure of this page is repurposed from the EzLicence refund policy and adapted for Driving School Liverpool Centre. It keeps the useful parts of the source, including the cooling-off concept, practical eligibility examples, exclusions and refund timing, while shifting the wording into a Liverpool branch context.

  • Refund requests are reviewed on the actual facts of the booking and payment.
  • Cooling-off and eligibility rules do not override obvious exclusions.
  • Related payment and cancellation pages still matter when a refund is reviewed.

Cooling-off period

A 48 hour cooling-off window may apply if no services have been booked

The source policy offers a 48 hour cooling-off period. Repurposed for the Liverpool branch, that means if a purchase is made, no services are booked and the person decides they are not ready to go ahead, a refund request made within 48 hours of the original transaction may qualify for a no-questions-asked refund.

When a refund may be available

Examples of possible refund eligibility

The source policy gives practical examples rather than vague promises. Repurposed for Driving School Liverpool Centre, examples of situations where a refund may be considered can include no available instructor servicing the learner’s location, no instructor availability in the next four weeks, accidental duplicate payments, leftover lesson credits after a large number of completed hours, an instructor not arriving, or an instructor cancelling the booking.

  • No available instructor servicing the relevant location.
  • No instructor availability within the next four weeks.
  • Accidental duplicate payment processing.
  • Unused remaining lesson credits after substantial completed bookings.
  • Instructor non-arrival or instructor cancellation.

Payment provider limits

Some payment methods can still block otherwise valid refunds

The source policy highlights that some payment providers impose their own time limits. Specifically, Afterpay transactions older than 120 days and PayPal transactions older than 180 days may no longer be refundable, even where a person might otherwise qualify under the refund policy. Those provider limits sit outside the driving school’s direct control.

When a refund is usually not available

Common exclusions need to be understood before paying

The source policy also explains a wide group of situations where a refund would usually not be available. Repurposed for the Liverpool branch, common examples include change of mind, expired or zero wallet value, preferred instructor unavailability where other instructors are available, general dissatisfaction that should instead be handled through rating or instructor change options, user booking errors, late cancellation charges, active payment disputes and non-refundable processing fees.

  • Change of mind, including finding the service cheaper or no longer wishing to use it.
  • User-caused booking problems, such as wrong service, wrong pickup address or wrong contact number.
  • Late cancellation charges already applied under the cancellation rules.
  • Platform processing fees that are stated as non-refundable.

Gift vouchers

Unredeemed gift voucher logic should be treated differently from used credit

The source policy states that gift vouchers are valid for 36 months and that a valid, unredeemed voucher may be refunded on request, while a voucher becomes non-refundable once it has been redeemed into a learner account. Repurposed for the Liverpool branch, the same logic is useful: unused voucher value can be handled differently from value that has already been converted into active learner credit.

Resolving requests

Approved refunds usually return to the original payment method in 5 to 10 business days

A refund request should be supported with as much information as possible to help review it properly. If a refund is approved, the source policy says it should generally return to the original payment method within 5 to 10 business days, depending on the bank or payment provider.

The source policy also makes clear that platform processing fees remain non-refundable, which means the final refunded amount may be less than the original total payment where those fees applied.

  • Provide clear booking and payment details with the request.
  • Approved refunds usually return to the original payment method.
  • Non-refundable processing fees can reduce the final refunded amount.
