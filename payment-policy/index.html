Policy overview

Payment should be clear before a learner commits.

A payment policy matters because lesson bookings often involve parents, younger learners, multi-hour packages and future test preparation. People need to know what they are paying for, when a discount applies, what happens if a booking changes and how refunds are handled if something goes wrong.

This page is repurposed from the EzLicence payment policy and adapted for Driving School Liverpool Centre. It keeps the useful structure of the source page but shifts the wording into a Liverpool branch context, with direct lesson pricing logic rather than platform-heavy support language.

  • Lesson or package price should be confirmed before payment is made.
  • Package discounts work by transaction, not by adding separate purchases together.
  • Cancellation and refund outcomes depend on the booking circumstances and policy rules.

Booking prices

Lesson prices can vary depending on the booking details

The source policy makes it clear that lesson prices are not always identical across every booking. In a practical Liverpool branch context, that means the final amount can vary depending on factors such as lesson duration, pickup arrangement, transmission type, package selection and the specific booking shown at checkout.

Visible before payment

The total lesson or package price should be shown and confirmed before payment is processed.

Different lesson structures

One-hour lessons, longer lessons and package purchases may not all be priced in the same way.

Local booking factors

Pickup requirements, transmission choice and the lesson arrangement itself can all affect the total amount shown.

Package discounts

How multi-hour discount bands are applied

The source policy uses a transaction-based discount structure. Repurposed for Driving School Liverpool Centre, the core idea is simple: if a learner buys multiple hours in one payment, a package discount may apply to those hours at the time of that transaction. That discount does not roll forward across separate future purchases.

The source structure describes three general tiers: 0 to 5 hours with no discount, 6 to 9 hours with a lower discount band, and 10 or more hours with a higher discount band. In practical terms, this means the package benefit applies to the bundle paid for at that time, not to unrelated later top-ups.

  • 0 to 5 booking hours in one transaction, no package discount band.
  • 6 to 9 booking hours in one transaction, lower package discount band.
  • 10 or more booking hours in one transaction, higher package discount band.

Processing and related fees

If a platform or transaction fee applies, it should be disclosed before payment

The EzLicence policy includes a non-refundable platform processing fee. For Driving School Liverpool Centre, the practical rule is that any platform, processing or checkout-related fee should be shown before payment so the learner or parent understands the total amount being charged.

Cancellations and validation

Booking changes can affect how payment is applied

The source policy links late cancellations directly to payment handling. Repurposed for the Liverpool branch, the key point is this: if a cancellation happens within 24 hours of the lesson start time, the payment outcome may depend on the late cancellation rule and the specific handling of that booking.

The policy also supports the idea that payment is generally treated as completed once a booked lesson reaches its end time, unless it is later reversed under the relevant booking and dispute rules. That matters because learners sometimes assume every post-lesson issue automatically becomes a refund, when in practice it needs to be reviewed against the booking record first.

  • Late cancellation means within 24 hours of the lesson start time.
  • Cancellation outcomes should be read alongside the separate cancellation policy.
  • Lesson payment may be credited, charged or reviewed depending on what actually happened.

Refund handling

Refunds are reviewed against the booking circumstances, not assumed automatically

The source policy makes clear that refund requests may be investigated and determined on their own facts. Repurposed for Driving School Liverpool Centre, that means a refund request may be reviewed where there is a payment issue, a service problem, an instructor-side fault or another verified concern connected to the booking.

Payment issues

Refunds may be considered where a payment problem or unauthorised transaction is identified.

Service-side fault

If a learner did not receive the lesson because of an instructor-side problem, the payment outcome may need to be reviewed.

Complaint review

If a complaint is raised and a conduct or service issue is verified, refund handling may be considered in line with the broader policy framework.
